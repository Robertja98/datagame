# Cloudy Mountain Reference

This file captures the narrative and interface principles guiding the quest-mode remake.

## Canonical Story Shape

- The player controls a three-member expedition that begins at a lone cabin.
- The objective is to recover the Crown of Kings from Cloudy Mountain.
- The route is blocked by wilderness obstacles and mountain-specific barriers.
- Earlier mountains are entered to recover the tools needed to open later routes.
- The quest escalates from preparation and survival to the final dragon confrontation.

## Progression Model

- Mountain 1 leads to the Brass Key.
- Mountain 2 leads to the River Boat.
- Mountain 3 leads to the Sun Axe.
- Cloudy Mountain is the final chapter.
- Each relic should feel like a chapter completion, not just a stat change.
- Winning the crown should create a return-home phase instead of an abrupt underground ending.

## UI Narrative Rules

- The overworld is the strategic story layer: preparation, route choice, and risk.
- The dungeon is the sensory story layer: darkness, partial knowledge, and immediate danger.
- Sound-before-sight should matter whenever possible.
- The interface should imply danger and discovery instead of explaining everything directly.
- Item and progression feedback can be dramatic, but routine HUD text should stay terse.

## Action-Clone Guidance

- Preserve overworld-to-dungeon transitions as a major story beat.
- Use each forced return to the wilderness as a chapter break.
- Emphasize arrows, health, and movement as survival resources.
- Let monsters feel different by motion, silhouette, and audio cues.
- Treat relics and the crown as meaningful expedition milestones.

## Tone

- The cabin is safety.
- The mountains are preparation, risk, and attrition.
- Cloudy Mountain is the final descent.
- The game should feel like the world is learned through surviving it, not through exposition.
